Hick's Law

The Giving Cookie

Hick's Law is exemplified in this website because there isn't an abundancce of choices for the consumer to make, there are a select amount of options, allowing the consumer to make a choice decision in a timely manner.

Fitt's Law

"Fitts’ law states that the amount of time required for a person to move a pointer (e.g., mouse cursor) to a target area is a function of the distance to the target divided by the size of the target. Thus, the longer the distance and the smaller the target’s size, the longer it takes." (Interaction Design Foundation) The irs.gov website demonstrates Fitt's law in an exceptional way as they made the size of their links a size large enough for a user to have a good range around the buttons without having to take additional time to move a curser over the button, allowing them ample time to click and go to their desired destination.

White Space and Clean Design

Wealthsimple

White Space and clean design is applied to this web page with the purpose of keeping a simple design that still captures the readers attention. It is not over crowded with information that confuses the user, rather there is plenty of white space between the headers and their corresponding text.
